Understanding the Bayesian Estimation Engine

Hooray! By shifting from a classical point-estimation approach to a Bayesian framework, we gain immense analytical advantages when studying continuous-time stochastic systems like the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process.

Advantages of Joint Volatility Estimation

  • Robust Error Correction: Classical methods assume $\sigma$ is fixed when estimating $\mu$. Our joint estimator handles the structural covariance between parameters natively.
  • Information Conservation: Instead of discarding historical volatility samples, the Inverse-Gamma posterior acts as an optimal recursive filter for variance.
  • Fast Convergence: We bypass costly Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) simulations entirely, maintaining lightning-fast continuous update rates using exact analytical conjugacy.

Math Behind Conjugate Volatility Updates

Given the discretization step deviation $e_t = X_{t+1} - X_t - \theta(\mathbb{E}[\mu] - X_t)\Delta t$, we have: e_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2 \Delta t) By setting an Inverse-Gamma prior on $V = \sigma^2$, we update the hyper-parameters at each step recursively: \alpha_t = \alpha_{t-1} + 0.5,\quad \beta_t = \beta_{t-1} + 0.5 \frac{e_t^2}{\Delta t} This allows us to instantly map the probability density of the volatility as $f(\sigma) = 2 \sigma f_V(\sigma^2)$.
